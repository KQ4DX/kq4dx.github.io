What is an FRN?

The FRN (Federal Registration Number) is a unique 10-digit number assigned by the FCC that identifies you in their database. It's required for all amateur radio license applications and is free to obtain.

How to Get Your FRN:

  1. Visit the FCC CORES website
  2. Click "Register" to create a new account
  3. Fill out your personal information accurately
  4. Verify your email address
  5. Your FRN will be assigned automatically

Important: Use your legal name exactly as it appears on your government-issued ID. This information must match what you bring to the exam session.

Get Your FRN at FCC CORES
Already have an amateur radio license? You can skip the FRN registration since you already have one from your original license application.
